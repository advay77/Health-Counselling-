Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about medical admissions

What is NEET counselling?

NEET counselling is the process through which qualified candidates are allocated seats in medical and dental colleges based on their NEET scores, preferences, and available seats. It involves registration, choice filling, seat allotment, and reporting to the allotted institution.

How can I get admission through NRI quota?

NRI quota admissions require you to be an NRI, child of NRI, or sponsored by an NRI relative. You need to qualify NEET with the minimum required percentile, submit necessary documents proving NRI status, and apply to colleges offering NRI quota seats. Our consultants can guide you through the specific requirements and process.

What is the difference between AIQ and State Quota?

All India Quota (AIQ) reserves 15% of seats in government medical colleges (except in J&K) for candidates from across India. State Quota comprises 85% of seats in government colleges, which are filled by respective state counselling authorities for candidates who belong to that state. Different states have different domicile criteria.
